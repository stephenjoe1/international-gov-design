Helsinki Conference 2024

1–2 October 2024

Central Helsinki and Espoo

Format

From 1 to 2 October 2024, we’re running a 2-day in-person conference in Helsinki and neighbouring Espoo. We are currently co-creating the programme with input from members of the International Design in Government community. It will be a multi-track event with keynotes and parallel sessions.

The conference will be adjacent to the Service Design Global Conference that will happen in Helsinki from 3 to 4 October.

The event is co-organised by Julkis-muotoilijat, the Finnish designers in government community, Aalto University, Estonia and the City of Tallinn, the City of Malmö and the City of Helsinki.

Participation

The Helsinki 2024 Conference is open and free to attend for any member of the International Design in Government community. Tickets will be released in small batches starting in May.

A call for ideas has closed now. From early May, there will be a call for proposals for talks, workshops and other formats.

If you work in government and you’d like to join the community, the conference and monthly calls, you can apply to join the Slack group.

About the community

The International Design in Government community is a group of design-minded people from governments all over the world. The purpose is to learn from each other, share best practice and design patterns, and discuss shared challenges so that we can try and work on them together.

If you work in government and you’d like to join the community, the conference and monthly calls, you can apply to join the Slack group.

The community is open to designers, user researchers, content designers, accessibility experts and other design-minded people. In order to join, you must be working embedded in government at any level: national, state or city — this includes consultancies that contract solely for government.

Email the community management team if you have any questions.

This conference is co-organised by the Finnish Government design community Julkis-muotoilijat, Aalto University, Estonia and the City of Tallinn, the City of Malmö and the City of Helsinki with support from the UK Government Digital Service and the German government’s Digital Service.
